Some of the most influential Caesars during the imperium period

1. Augustus (27 BC – AD 14) was the first Roman emperor, overseeing the transformation of Rome from a republic to an empire.
2. Hadrian (AD 117 – 138) focused on consolidating the empire’s borders, commissioning the construction of Hadrian’s Wall in Britain.
3. Constantine the Great (AD 306 – 337) was the first Roman emperor to convert to Christianity and established Constantinople as the empire's new capital.
4. Trajan (AD 98 – 117) expanded the Roman Empire to its greatest territorial extent, conquering Dacia and commissioning major architectural works like Trajan's Column.
5. Diocletian (AD 284 – 305) stabilized the empire through reforms, creating the Tetrarchy to manage its vast territories, and initiated the last major persecution of Christians.
6. Marcus Aurelius (AD 161 – 180), a Stoic philosopher, led Rome through the Marcomannic Wars and is remembered for his philosophical writings in the "Meditations."
